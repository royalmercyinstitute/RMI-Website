About Royal Mercy Institute

Our Story

Founded 2020
Who We Are
Royal Mercy Institute is a South African science and technology enterprise founded in 2020 by Mohau Mokoena (CEO & CTO). Our mission is to make quality STEM education accessible to young people aged 10–25 from disadvantaged and under-resourced communities across South Africa.

We are currently in the empathy phase, with our app (FlutterFlow) and website (Wix) actively in development. We compete in the MTN Digital Solutions Competition hosted by Enactus South Africa under SDG 4: Quality Education — we made it to the Top 40 finalists, competing against universities from across South Africa as Enactus NWU (North-West University).
The Problem
Why We Exist
South Africa faces a critical shortage of STEM skills. Young people from disadvantaged backgrounds face barriers including lack of data, devices, and locally relevant content. Existing global platforms like YouTube, Udemy and Khan Academy are too expensive, require reliable internet, or are not designed for the SA curriculum.

Without intervention, these learners risk exclusion from the digital economy entirely — perpetuating cycles of poverty and inequality.
🎯
Our Mission
To make quality, locally-relevant STEM education accessible to every South African learner, regardless of socioeconomic background.
👁
Our Vision
To become the leading STEM education and innovation platform for disadvantaged communities in South Africa — and across Africa.
⚡
Competitive Edge
Built specifically for SA disadvantaged communities. Locally contextualised content. Low-bandwidth mobile optimised. Mission-driven, not profit-driven.
🏆
Competition
Competing in the MTN Digital Solutions Competition hosted by Enactus South Africa. SDG 4: Quality Education sector. Empathy phase currently active.

Level 2 Intermediate

JWT Auth + Role-Based Access + Database

🔐 Authentication Flow

How JWT Auth Works:
  1. User submits email + password to /api/auth/login
  2. Server verifies password with bcrypt.compare()
  3. Server signs a JWT with user ID, email, role
  4. Token stored client-side (localStorage)
  5. Subsequent requests include Authorization: Bearer <token>
  6. Middleware verifies token on protected routes
  7. Role check: admin vs learner access
GET/api/users🔒 Admin Only
// Fetches all users — admin JWT required
